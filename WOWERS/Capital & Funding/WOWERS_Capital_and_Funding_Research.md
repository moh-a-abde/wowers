### Detailed Cost Categories

#### A. Micro-Hydropower Turbine Manufacturing
- **Ossberger crossflow (Germany):** $50K-$150K turbine-generator package
- **CINK Hydro-Energy (Czech):** $40K-$90K 
- **Canyon Hydro (USA):** $80K-$200K for complete package
- **Kaplan turbines (more expensive):** $100K-$250K

At volume (50+ units), manufacturing cost drops to **$25K-$50K per unit**.

#### B. 316L Stainless Steel Fabrication
- **Material cost:** $3.50-$5.50/lb ($7.7K-$12.1K per metric ton)
- **Housing + runner fabrication:** $30K-$60K domestically, $18K-$35K from Czech/India
- **Investment casting for runners:** $8K-$25K depending on complexity

#### C. SCADA System Integration
- **Per-unit hardware (PLC, sensors, flow meters, power metering):** $12K-$25K
- **Cloud platform development (one-time):** $80K-$200K
- **Annual hosting/maintenance:** $10K-$30K/year
- **Alternative:** Subscription-based SCADA ($200-$500/month/asset)

#### D. Engineering & Prototyping
- **CFD analysis:** $15K-$50K
- **FEA (structural/fatigue):** $10K-$30K
- **First-article prototype:** $80K-$200K (crossflow), $120K-$300K (Kaplan)
- **Prototype testing:** $30K-$80K
- **Total engineering through validated prototype:** $200K-$500K

#### E. Regulatory & Compliance
- **FERC Conduit Exemption filing:** $5K-$20K in attorney fees (fee itself is $0 for qualifying facilities)
- **EPA NPDES permit modifications:** $2K-$10K
- **UL/ETL certification:** $20K-$50K
- **IEEE 1547 interconnection testing:** $10K-$30K
- **Per-installation regulatory:** $40K-$100K (first site), $10K-$30K each subsequent

#### F. Patents & IP
- **Provisional patent:** $7K-$16K (attorney + filing)
- **Full utility patent:** $25K-$50K (through issuance)
- **Freedom-to-operate search:** $5K-$15K
- **International PCT application:** $15K-$25K
- **Recommended 2-year IP budget:** $50K-$80K

#### G. Insurance (Year 1)
- **Product liability ($1-2M coverage):** $8K-$20K
- **Professional liability:** $5K-$15K
- **General commercial:** $3K-$8K
- **Installation contractors liability:** $10K-$25K
- **Total Year 1:** $25K-$60K

Note: Municipalities will require $5-10M aggregate liability with them as additional insured (increases premiums significantly).

## 3. FEDERAL GRANT & LOAN PROGRAMS

### A. DOE SBIR/STTR (Best Single Source)
- **Phase I:** $200K for 6-12 months feasibility study
- **Phase II:** $1.1M for 2 years full R&D
- **Eligibility:** U.S. small business (<500 employees). STTR requires university partner (St. Thomas qualifies)
- **Relevant topics:** Water Power Technologies Office publishes annual topics on micro-hydro and conduit hydropower
- **Application cycle:** Annual (typically fall release, spring deadline)
- **Recommendation:** STTR track with St. Thomas as research partner. Apply next cycle.

### B. EPA SBIR
- **Phase I:** ~$100K
- **Focus:** Wastewater treatment innovations
- **Fit:** Direct alignment with WOWERS

### C. EPA WIFIA (Water Infrastructure Finance & Innovation Act)
- **Type:** Low-interest loans (not grants), up to 49 years
- **Minimum project size:** $5M (small communities), $20M (large)
- **Eligibility:** Municipal utilities, state infrastructure authorities
- **WOWERS role:** Technology vendor/subcontractor. Municipalities bundle WOWERS into larger wastewater project.
- **Interest rate:** Locked at U.S. Treasury rate (below market)

### D. DOE Water Power Technologies Office Programs (Live as of 2026)
- **Hydropower Testing Network (HyTN) Voucher:** Opens March 12, 2026 (closes April 23). Provides testing facility access.
- **Hydropower Technical Collaboration Program:** Rolling applications, supports advanced manufacturing
- **Hydropower Collegiate Competition (2027):** Opens March 9, 2026. Direct fit for student team.

### E. IRA Clean Energy Tax Credits (Critical for Customers)
- **Investment Tax Credit (ITC), Section 48:** 30% of cost for hydropower with wage/apprenticeship compliance (6% base otherwise)
- **Direct Pay (Section 6417):** Tax-exempt municipalities can receive as cash payment instead of tax credit
- **Bonus credit:** Additional 10% for domestic content
- **WOWERS implication:** Your customers get 30% of installation cost back from federal government. Game-changer for payback period.

### F. USDA REAP (Rural Energy for America Program)
- **Grant:** Up to 25% of project costs, max $1M
- **Loan guarantee:** Up to $25M
- **Combined:** 75% cost coverage possible
- **Eligibility:** Rural small businesses (pop <50K), agricultural producers
- **Renewal:** 2 application windows/year (spring & fall)
- **Fit:** WOWERS targeting rural municipal wastewater plants

### G. Other Federal Programs
- **Bureau of Reclamation WaterSMART:** $200K-$5M grants for water/energy efficiency
- **EPA Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF):** Low-interest loans (1-2%) for wastewater infrastructure
- **FEMA BRIC:** If framed as resilience infrastructure (wastewater backup power)
- **Section 243 Hydroelectric Production Incentive:** $0.015/kWh for 10 years, up to $1M/year per facility
- **DOE Hydropower Prize competitions:** Periodic $50K-$500K prizes

## 8. KEY COST-SAVING STRATEGIES

1. **Leverage University Resources** (saves $150K-$300K)
   - Machine shops & CNC (save $50-100K on prototyping)
   - Hydraulics testing labs (save $20-50K on validation)
   - Software licenses free (SolidWorks, ANSYS, MATLAB)
   - Office/workshop in university incubator (free or $500/month vs $1.5-3K)
   - Faculty advisors as technical consultants (free vs $150-300/hr)

2. **Sub-Scale Prototype First** (costs 60-75% less)
   - Build 1/4 or 1/2 scale prototype first
   - Validates core engineering before full-scale build
   - Dramatically cheaper materials and machining

3. **Off-the-Shelf Components**
   - Use commercial generators, standard bearings, standard seals
   - Don't custom-design what already exists
   - Only innovate on system integration & wastewater-specific packaging

4. **Free/Discounted Pilot**
   - Offer first installation at cost (not $180K) in exchange for:
     - Site access and operational data
     - Customer testimonial for future sales
     - Case study for grant applications

5. **Stay Student/Part-Time** (saves $100K+)
   - Avoid taking salaries until grants/investor funding arrives
   - Most comparable companies took 2-4 years pre-revenue

6. **Provisional Patent Only** (saves $25K-$35K initially)
   - Provisional: $7K-$16K, gives 12 months of "patent pending"
   - File full utility patent later after pilot success/funding
